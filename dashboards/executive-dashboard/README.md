# Employee Experience Executive Dashboard

## 📊 Project Overview
This project transformed fragmented annual employee pulse surveys into a cohesive, longitudinal trend analysis tool. By integrating this data into SharePoint and coupling it with a "Weekly Wins" reporting pipeline, I created a high-visibility system used by the CEO and Executive Leadership to monitor departmental health and celebrate team achievements.

## 🔴 The Challenge
- Inconsistent Data: Annual surveys often changed questions year-to-year, making it nearly impossible to track long-term employee sentiment or retention trends.
- Low Visibility: Survey results were often "siloed" in static reports, offering little value for ongoing growth or immediate intervention.
- Communication Gap: The department lacked a formal, streamlined way to bubble up team successes to senior leadership

## 🛠️ The Solution
1. Longitudinal Survey Mapping (Power BI)
- Trend Analysis: I identified "core" evergreen questions across multiple years of pulse surveys, normalizing the data to create a consistent timeline.
- Drill-Down Functionality: Developed a dashboard allowing leaders to toggle between high-level departmental scores and specific team-level metrics to identify opportunities for growth.
- SharePoint Integration: Embedded the live dashboard into the company SharePoint site, ensuring "evergreen" access for all stakeholders.

2. The "Weekly Wins" Initiative
- Executive Reporting: Formalized a process to capture and curate team successes, creating a weekly reporting loop that reaches the CEO and Executive suite.
- Cultural Impact: Positioned these "wins" alongside the survey data to provide a holistic view of both employee sentiment and tangible performance.

## 🚀 Impact & Results
- Retention Strategy: Provided the data foundation for a new evergreen program aimed at improving employee retention and identifying "at-risk" areas.
- Executive Engagement: The "Weekly Wins" initiative significantly increased team visibility, directly influencing positive culture and recognition from the highest level of leadership.
- Accessibility: Moved the department from "once-a-year" data reviews to a continuous, self-service model of transparency.
